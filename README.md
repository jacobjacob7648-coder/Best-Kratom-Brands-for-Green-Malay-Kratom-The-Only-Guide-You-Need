## How to Spot a Bad Kratom Brand Fast

The market has too many bad actors. Here's a quick checklist to protect yourself:

**Red flags to avoid:**

- No AKA certification mentioned anywhere
- Lab reports are outdated or missing entirely
- Prices that seem impossibly cheap (under $8/oz)
- No physical address or contact info
- Reviews only on their own website
- No return or satisfaction guarantee
- Vague sourcing ("imported from Asia")

**Green flags to look for:**

- AKA GMP Certified badge (verifiable on AKA's website)
- Certificates of Analysis (COAs) per batch, publicly posted
- Named sourcing regions (Malaysian highlands, etc.)
- Independent reviews on Reddit's r/quittingkratom and r/kratom communities
- Active customer support via email and phone

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Green Malay kratom legal?**
Kratom is legal at the federal level in the US. However, it is banned in a handful of states including Alabama, Arkansas, Indiana, Rhode Island, Vermont, and Wisconsin. Always check your local laws.

**How long do Green Malay effects last?**
Most users report 5–8 hours of effects. This is longer than most other green vein strains — one of the key reasons it's so popular.

**Can I take Green Malay every day?**
Daily use increases tolerance and dependence risk. Most experienced users recommend 3–4 days per week maximum, with regular tolerance breaks.

**What's the best way to take Green Malay powder?**
The most common method is the "toss and wash" — measure your dose, place it on your tongue, wash it down with water. Some users mix it into orange juice to mask the taste.

**How do I verify a brand's AKA certification?**
Go directly to the American Kratom Association website at americankratom.org and search their vendor directory. Never trust a badge on a brand's own website without verifying it yourself.
